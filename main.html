Rosa Parks

African-American Activist of the Civil Rights Movements

The Timeline of Rosa Park's Life:

    • 1913 - Rosa Louise McCauley was born Feburary 4th, in Tuskegee, Alabama. Her mother Leona was a teacher, and her father James, was a carpenter.
    • 1932 - Rosa got married to Raymond Parks who was a barber from Montgomery. At this time she worked various jobs from a domestic worker to a hospital.
    • 1933 - By the strong encouragement from her husband, she was able to complete and finish highschool of this year. At this time, Raymond was a memeber of the NAACP (National Association for the Advancement of Colored People). Which was at the time supported the Scottsboro Boys, men who were falsely accused of raping two white women.
    • 1943 - Rosa became active in the Civil Rights Movement, joined the Montgomery chapter of the NAACP, and was elected secretary at a time when this was considered a woman's job. She said "I was the only woman there, and they needed a secretary, and I was too timid to say no." She continued as a secretary until 1957. She worked for the loal NAACP leader Edgar Nixon, even thought he maintained that "women don't need to be nowhere but in the kitchen".
    • 1955 - A bus driver verbally bullied and abused Rosa, along with three other black people for sitting in a "Whites Only" section. Rosa flat out refused, while the other three complied. She stated, "When he saw me still siting, he asked if I was going to stand up, and I said, 'No, I'm not.' And he said 'Well, if you don't stand up, I'm going to have to call the police and have you arrested.' I said, 'You may do that.'" She was then arrested by the police and charged.
    • 1955 - On December 4th plans for the Montgomery Bus Boycott was announced at the local Black-raced churches. At a church rally that night, those attending agree unanimously to continue the boycott until they were treated with the level of courtesy they expected, until black drivers were hired. And until seating in the middle of the bus was handled on a first-come basis. The next day she went to her trial tht only lasted 30minutes. She was found guilty and fined $10.
    • 1970 - Rosa organized for the freedom of political prisioners in the United States, particularly cases involving issues of self-defense.
    • 1977 - This was unfortunately one of the saddest years for Rosa. On August 19th, her husband Raymond died of throat cancer. That November, her only brother died of cancer. From reading the papers, she found of an old friend from the past died as well. Rosa herself at this time suffered two broken bones white caused considerable and recurring pain.
    • 1972 - Rosa decided to move in with her mother into an apartment for senior citizens. There she nursed her mother Leona through the final stages of cancer and geriatric dementia until she died in 1979 at the age of 92.
    • 1980 - Parks—widowed and without immediate family—rededicated herself to civil rights and educational organizations. She co-founded the Rosa L. Parks Scholarship Foundation for college-bound high school seniors, to which she donated most of her speaker fees.
    • 1987 - In February 1987, she co-founded, with Elaine Eason Steele, the Rosa and Raymond Parks Institute for Self Development, an institute that runs the "Pathways to Freedom" bus tours which introduce young people to important civil rights and Underground Railroad sites throughout the country. Parks also served on the Board of Advocates of Planned Parenthood. Though her health declined as she entered her seventies, Parks continued to make many appearances and devoted considerable energy to these causes.
    • 1992 - Parks published Rosa Parks: My Story, an autobiography aimed at younger readers, which recounts her life leading to her decision to keep her seat on the bus. A few years later, she published Quiet Strength (1995), her memoir, which focuses on her faith.
    • 1999 - Parks filmed a cameo appearance for the television series Touched by an Angel. It was her last appearance on film; Parks began to suffer from health problems due to old age.
    • 2005 - Parks died of natural causes on October 24, at the age of 92, in her apartment on the east side of Detroit. She and her husband never had children and she outlived her only sibling. She was survived by her sister-in-law (Raymond's sister), 13 nieces and nephews and their families, and several cousins, most of them residents of Michigan or Alabama.

    “I had decided that I would not go anywhere with a piece of paper in my hand asking white folks for any favors. I had made that decision myself, as an individual.”

    - Rosa Parks, Rosa Parks: My Story
